LLM Summary: Malayan Banking Berhad, New York Branch (MBBNY) is presenting its first annual U.S. Resolution Plan, a public section, as required by the Dodd-Frank Act. This plan outlines a strategy for the rapid and orderly resolution of MBBNY's U.S. operations in the event of financial distress. MBBNY, a wholesale branch licensed by the New York State Department of Financial Services (NYSDFS) since September 1984, operates alongside its subsidiary, Maybank Kim Eng Securities USA Inc. (MKEUSA), a broker-dealer licensed by FINRA since 1991. As of December 31, 2012, the parent company, Malayan Banking Berhad (MBBG), headquartered in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia, was the largest bank in Malaysia and a significant financial institution in the Asia Pacific, with total assets of USD$161 billion, employing 47,233 people globally across 2,200 branches in 20 countries. MBBNY itself had 33 employees and USD$942 million in assets, while MKEUSA had 18 employees and USD$2.9 million in assets. MBBNY's core business lines are Global Markets (including wholesale deposits, repurchase agreements, investment portfolio, and derivatives) and Corporate Banking & Client Coverage (including wholesale lending and secured/unsecured loans). The branch's financial information as of December 31, 2012, shows total assets of USD$942.23 million and total liabilities of USD$942.23 million, funded primarily by wholesale deposits, medium-term funding from Head Office, and collateralized corporate funding (Repos). MBBNY utilizes derivative activities like Interest Rate Swaps (IRS), Overnight Index Swap (OIS), and Cross Currency Swaps for hedging. The branch has clearing arrangements with major U.S. correspondent banks, including Fedline Advantage for funds transfer services and JP Morgan Access for U.S. dollar trade settlements. MBBNY is regulated by the Federal Reserve Bank of New York (FRBNY) and licensed by NYSDFS; its deposits are not FDIC insured. The resolution plan development is overseen by various committees, including the Asset Recovery & Disposal Committee, Liquidity and Funding Committee, Business Continuity Committee, and Finance Committee. MBBNY's resolution strategy assumes an unforeseen single large adverse event, potentially impacting Head Office financial stress, which could lead to the liquidation of assets in line with the Living Will plan, aiming for an orderly resolution with no systemic impact on U.S. financial markets.
